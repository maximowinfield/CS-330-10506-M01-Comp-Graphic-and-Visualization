# CS-330-10506-M01-Comp-Graphic-and-Visualization
Coursework from CS-330 Computer Graphics &amp; Visualization at SNHU. Features 3D rendering using C++ and OpenGL, including transformations, lighting, textures, and camera controls to build interactive scenes and demonstrate core graphics programming concepts.


Reflection

How do I approach designing software?
I approach software design by first understanding the goal of the system and then breaking it down into smaller, manageable components. For this project, I focused on how each part of the 3D scene—objects, lighting, textures, and camera—would work together. I aimed to keep the design modular so each element could be adjusted without affecting the entire scene.

What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
This project helped me develop a better understanding of spatial design and how different elements interact in a 3D environment. I learned how to structure scenes using transformations and how lighting and textures impact realism. It also improved my ability to think visually while still applying logical programming structure.

What design process did you follow for your project work?
I followed an iterative design process where I started with a basic scene and gradually added complexity. I first ensured objects rendered correctly, then applied transformations, followed by lighting and textures. At each step, I tested and adjusted before moving forward to avoid compounding errors.

How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?
The step-by-step and modular approach I used can be applied to any software project. Breaking problems into smaller parts and validating each stage before moving forward helps reduce bugs and makes systems easier to maintain and expand.

How do I approach developing programs?
I approach development by building incrementally and testing frequently. Instead of trying to complete everything at once, I focus on getting one feature working correctly before moving to the next. This helps me stay organized and makes debugging more manageable.

What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?
One key strategy I used was isolating individual components, such as testing transformations or lighting separately before combining them. I also relied more on visual feedback, adjusting values in real time to understand how changes affected the scene.

How did iteration factor into your development?
Iteration was a major part of my development process. I continuously refined object placement, lighting intensity, and camera positioning. Each iteration improved the overall quality of the scene and helped me better understand how small adjustments can significantly impact the final result.

How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?
At the beginning, I focused mainly on getting code to work. As I progressed, I became more intentional about structure, readability, and reusability. By the end of the project, I was thinking more about how to organize code cleanly and make it easier to modify or extend.

How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?
Computer science provides me with the skills to build real-world applications and solve complex problems. It supports my goal of becoming a software developer by strengthening my ability to think logically, design systems, and create scalable solutions.

How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills for your educational pathway?
Working with computational graphics has expanded my understanding of how math, logic, and programming come together in a visual context. It has strengthened my problem-solving skills and introduced concepts like transformations and rendering that can support further study in graphics or simulation.

How do computational graphics and visualizations apply to your future professional pathway?
These skills are valuable in areas such as game development, simulation, and user interface design. Understanding how to create and manipulate visual systems gives me an advantage in building interactive applications and enhances my overall versatility as a developer.
